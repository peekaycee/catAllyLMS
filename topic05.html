Cat Health

When to see a Vet

Ensuring the health and well-being of your cat is paramount for their longevity and quality of life. Regular veterinary care and attentive observation are crucial components of maintaining your cat's health. Here's a deeper dive into cat health and when to see a vet.

Regular Veterinary Check-ups: Schedule routine veterinary visits for your cat, even if they appear healthy. Annual check-ups allow the vet to assess your cat's overall health, administer vaccinations, and discuss preventive care measures. Follow your veterinarian's recommendations for parasite prevention, such as flea, tick, and heartworm control, as well as routine deworming.

Monitoring Your Cat's Health: Keep an eye on your cat's appetite, thirst, urination, and bowel movements. Changes in these behaviors could indicate underlying health problems. Monitor your cat's weight and body condition. Sudden weight loss or gain could be a sign of illness. Regularly inspect your cat's fur, skin, eyes, ears, and mouth for any abnormalities, such as lumps, wounds, discharge, or dental issues.

Common Signs of Illness in Cats:

Persistent vomiting or diarrhea.

Difficulty breathing or coughing.

Lethargy or weakness.

Loss of appetite or sudden weight loss.

Excessive thirst or urination.

Changes in litter box habits, such as straining to urinate or blood in the urine.

Persistent sneezing, coughing, or nasal discharge.

Visible signs of pain or discomfort, such as limping or vocalization.
